Celebrate Women's History Month at Syntax

This March, The New York Public Library celebrates women, past and present, who have been active in all forms of media and storytelling in honor of Women's History Month.

Join us for recommended reading, programs, resources, and more. Plus, check out book lists that highlight the contributions of women writers and look back on the women who helped build NYPL.

Welcome to Syntax Squad Library

The library was founded in 2023 by a group of four individuals passionate about knowledge and education: Robert Neszt, Ruiqing Zhu, Nadia Mikhaylova, and Shubhparteek Singh.

Together, Robert, Ruiqing, Nadia, and Shubhparteek work tirelessly to ensure that the library is a welcoming and inclusive space for everyone to explore, learn, and grow.

Upcoming Events:

Author Talk: Margaret Atwood

Join us for an evening with Margaret Atwood, author of The Handmaid's Tale and many other beloved works of fiction and poetry. Atwood will discuss her latest book, The Testaments, and take questions from the audience.

Movie Night: Casablanca

Come enjoy a classic film on the big screen! We'll be showing Casablanca, the 1942 romantic drama starring Humphrey Bogart and Ingrid Bergman. Refreshments will be provided.

Children's Storytime

Bring your little ones to the library for a fun morning of stories, songs, and crafts! We'll read some of our favorite books and make some special crafts to take home.
